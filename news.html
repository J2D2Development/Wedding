Schedule of Events

Thursday, September 10
We are going to arrange a casual meet and greet at a bar in North Portland. Check back here for more details closer to the date.

Friday, September 11 during the day
For out-of-town guests, there is an endless list of ways to enjoy the summer in Oregon. A few suggestions are as follows
  • A hike in the gorge: Ashley recently did a 6 mile round trip hike to Burnt Lake and loved it! She suggests bringing a sack lunch and your swimming suit.
  • Another hike in the gorge: Multnomah Falls never disappoints! It is a short drive from downtown Portland. For those interested in more of a hike, try the Wahkeena Falls Loop.
  • Visit Hood River: Hood River is a beautiful hour-long drive from Portland. It is a great spot for having lunch, exploring the town, and sampling some great beers. Some of our favorite breweries are Double Mountain, Pfriem, Full Sail . . . and our absolute favorite. . . Everybody's Brewery. Everybody's is located across the bridge in Washington State. Added bonus! Drive back to Portland on the Washington side of the gorge! Worth it.
    Editor's note: only worth it if you don't get stuck behind a slow moving truck. The Washington side route is a 2 lane road for about 50 miles.
  • Visit Timberline Lodge: Located 1.5 hours from Portland and the set for the movie The Shining. This is a great place to go for lunch and take in some amazing views.
  • Go surfing! Although this may be the only weekend of the summer that Paul Boyle won't be able to give you a personal tour and lesson (sorry Dad!) you should still visit the Boyle parent's home-away-from home Pacific City. Explore the beach, freeze to death in the water, and grab a beer at the Pelican Pub. If you want to try your luck at the waves, there are at least two great rental shops in town.
  • Stay in Portland. Experience Portlandia! Go to Powell's Books, walk around the 23rd, SE Hawthorne, the International Test Rose Gardens or North Portland's Mississippi and Williams Streets. Make sure to sample some great coffee, vegan fare, and--obviously--stop at one of the bazillions of pubs.
  • Also, for those of you staying in NW Portland, you must visit Forest Park and go for a walk/hike/run. It is Portland's not-so-hidden jewel.
  • Visit wine country! We actually have no recommendations on this activity. But we hear it is just GREAT!

Transportation

We have arranged a shuttle from the NW Holiday Inn Express (2333 NW Vaughn St, Portland, Oregon). If you are reserving a hotel, we strongly urge you to stay at one of these locations and take advantage of the shuttle.

On September 12, the shuttles will leave the Holiday Inn Express at 2:45pm and 3:50pm. The shuttle will then make continuous trips from Bladberry Farm back to the hotels starting at 8:30pm with the last shuttle leaving at 10:30pm.

Appropriate attire

Judging from this summer's heat, it may be a scorcher! The location is almost entirely grass, with a small indoor barn. For ladies, we would discourage heels. For gentlemen, ties are encouraged, jackets are not.

There will be a scattering of games--and this is a competitive bunch--so you may want to pick an ensemble that will allow you to throw a mean beanbag.
